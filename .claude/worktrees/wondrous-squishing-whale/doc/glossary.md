# Glossary of Dutch Legal Terms

This glossary defines Dutch legal and administrative terms used throughout the
RFCs and codebase. Terms are grouped by domain. Within prose, RFCs use the
convention **English term** (*Dutch term*) on **every** occurrence, not just
the first. This ensures readers who skim or jump into the middle of a document
can always connect English descriptions to their precise Dutch legal equivalents.

For YAML field names, enum values, and quoted legal text, the original Dutch is
preserved — these are technical identifiers or verbatim law and should not be
translated.

## Legal Hierarchy

| English | Dutch | Definition |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Constitution | *Grondwet* | The Dutch Constitution, highest level of national law. |
| Formal law / statute | *wet* | Primary legislation passed by Parliament (Eerste and Tweede Kamer). Example: Wet op de zorgtoeslag. |
| General administrative order | *Algemene Maatregel van Bestuur* (AMvB) | Government decree, one level below a formal law. Issued by the Crown on recommendation of the Council of State. |
| Ministerial regulation | *ministeriële regeling* | Regulation issued by a minister to implement details delegated by a formal law or AMvB. |
| Municipal ordinance | *gemeentelijke verordening* | Regulation issued by a municipal council (*gemeenteraad*). |
| Royal decree | *Koninklijk Besluit* (KB) | Decision issued by the Crown (the King together with one or more ministers). |
| EU regulation | *EU verordening* | Directly applicable EU-level regulation. |
| Policy rule | *beleidsregel* | Guideline published by an administrative body on how it exercises a discretionary power. Not directly binding on courts but creates legitimate expectations. |

## Administrative Law Concepts

| English | Dutch | Definition |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Administrative decision | *besluit* | A written decision by an administrative body (*bestuursorgaan*) with external legal effect (AWB 1:3). The central concept of Dutch administrative law. |
| Individual decision | *beschikking* | An administrative decision (*besluit*) directed at one or more specific individuals, as opposed to a generally applicable rule (AWB 1:3 lid 2). Includes both grants (*toekenningen*) and rejections (*afwijzingen*). |
| General rule | *besluit van algemene strekking* | An administrative decision that applies generally (e.g., a regulation or policy rule), not directed at specific individuals. |
| Assessment | *toets* | Evaluation of whether legal conditions are met. |
| Grant / award | *toekenning* | Positive decision granting a benefit or right. |
| Rejection | *afwijzing* | Negative decision denying an application. |
| Determination | *vaststelling* | Formal establishment of facts or amounts by an administrative body. |
| Authority / competence | *bevoegdheid* | The legal power of an administrative body to make binding decisions in a specific area. |
| Delegation of authority | *mandaat / delegatie* | Transfer of decision-making power. *Mandaat*: exercised on behalf of the delegating body. *Delegatie*: transferred entirely. |
| Reasoning requirement | *motiveringsplicht* | Legal obligation for an administrative body to provide adequate reasoning for its decisions (AWB 3:46). |
| Lex specialis | *lex specialis* | Legal principle: a more specific law takes precedence over a general one. In Dutch legal text signaled by "*in afwijking van*" (in derogation of). |
| Lex posterior | *lex posterior* | Legal principle: a newer law takes precedence over an older one of the same level. |
| Lex superior | *lex superior* | Legal principle: a higher-level law takes precedence over a lower-level one. |

## Administrative Procedure (AWB)

| English | Dutch | Definition |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Application | *aanvraag* | Formal request submitted by an interested party (*belanghebbende*) to an administrative body for a decision (AWB 4:1). |
| Processing / examination | *behandeling* | Investigation phase during which the administrative body examines the application. |
| Decision deadline | *beslistermijn* | Statutory deadline within which the administrative body must issue a decision. Default: "reasonable period," typically 8 weeks (AWB 4:13). |
| Notification / publication | *bekendmaking* | Communication of the decision to the affected party. For a permit (*beschikking*): by mail or delivery. For general rules: publication in an official gazette (AWB 3:41). |
| Objection | *bezwaar* | Formal objection filed with the administrative body that issued the decision, requesting reconsideration (AWB 6:4). |
| Objection period | *bezwaartermijn* | Deadline for filing an objection: six weeks from the day after notification (AWB 6:7–6:8). |
| Decision on objection | *besluit op bezwaar* (BOB) | New decision issued after reconsideration of the original decision following an objection (AWB 7:12). |
| Appeal | *beroep* | Appeal to an administrative court (*bestuursrechter*) against a decision or a decision on objection (AWB 8:1). |
| Higher appeal | *hoger beroep* | Appeal to a higher court against the ruling of the first-instance administrative court (AWB 8:104). |
| Hearing | *horen* | Obligation to hear the objecting party before deciding on the objection (AWB 7:2). |
| Reconsideration | *heroverweging* | Full reconsideration of the original decision on its merits during the objection phase (AWB 7:11). |
| Interim relief | *voorlopige voorziening* | Temporary court measure to prevent irreversible harm while an objection or appeal is pending (AWB 8:81). |
| Short-circuit ruling | *kortsluiting* | The interim relief judge immediately decides the main case instead of only the provisional request (AWB 8:86). |
| Penalty for late decision | *dwangsom* | Financial penalty accruing daily when an administrative body fails to decide within the statutory deadline (AWB 4:17). |
| Notice of default | *ingebrekestelling* | Formal written notice from the applicant that the administrative body has exceeded the decision deadline (AWB 4:17). |
| Administrative repair loop | *bestuurlijke lus* | Procedure during appeal where the court identifies a defect and gives the administrative body a deadline to repair it, suspending the appeal in the meantime (AWB 8:51a). |
| Withdrawal / revocation | *intrekking* | Withdrawal of a previously issued decision by the administrative body (AWB 10:4–10:5). |
| Final / irrevocable | *onherroepelijk* | State where a decision can no longer be challenged through regular legal remedies. |
| Admissibility check | *ontvankelijkheidstoets* | Preliminary check whether an objection or appeal meets formal requirements (filed on time, by an interested party, against a proper decision). |
| Direct appeal (skipping objection) | *rechtstreeks beroep* | Procedure where the objection phase is skipped and the case goes directly to the administrative court (AWB 7:1a). |
| Residence permit | *verblijfsvergunning* | Permit granting a foreign national the right to reside in the Netherlands, issued under the Aliens Act (*Vreemdelingenwet*). |

## Uniform Public Preparation Procedure (UOV)

| English | Dutch | Definition |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Uniform public preparation procedure | *uniforme openbare voorbereidingsprocedure* (UOV) | Alternative preparation procedure with public consultation instead of individual hearing. When applied, objection (*bezwaar*) is excluded; legal protection goes directly to appeal (*beroep*) (AWB afdeling 3.4). |
| Draft decision | *ontwerpbesluit* | Preliminary version of the decision made available for public inspection. |
| Public inspection | *terinzagelegging* | Period (six weeks) during which the draft decision and supporting documents are available for inspection (AWB 3:11–3:14). |
| Submitted views | *zienswijzen* | Formal comments submitted by the public during the inspection period (AWB 3:15–3:17). |

## Government Bodies and Roles

| English | Dutch | Definition |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Administrative body | *bestuursorgaan* | Any government body that exercises public authority and can issue administrative decisions (AWB 1:1). |
| Interested party | *belanghebbende* | A person or entity whose interests are directly affected by an administrative decision (AWB 1:2). |
| Municipality | *gemeente* | Local government unit, governed by a municipal council (*gemeenteraad*) and an executive board (*college van burgemeester en wethouders*). |
| College of Mayor and Aldermen | *college van burgemeester en wethouders* (college van B&W) | Executive board of a municipality. Competent authority for many municipal decisions. |
| Municipal council | *gemeenteraad* | Legislative body of a municipality. Issues municipal ordinances (*verordeningen*). |
| Province | *provincie* | Regional government unit between national and municipal level. |
| Water board | *waterschap* | Special-purpose authority responsible for water management in a designated area. |
| Crown | *Kroon* | The King acting together with the responsible minister(s). Issues royal decrees (*Koninklijke Besluiten*). |
| Administrative court | *bestuursrechter* | Judge specialized in disputes between citizens and administrative bodies. |
| Allowances Service | *Dienst Toeslagen* | Executive agency of the Ministry of Finance responsible for administering income-dependent allowances (*toeslagen*): healthcare allowance (*zorgtoeslag*), rent allowance (*huurtoeslag*), childcare allowance (*kinderopvangtoeslag*), and child-related budget (*kindgebonden budget*). |
| Tax Authority | *Belastingdienst* | National tax administration. Also the source holder (*bronhouder*) of the Income Registry (*Basisregistratie Inkomen*, BRI). |
| Tax inspector | *inspecteur* | Official within the Tax Authority (*Belastingdienst*) who formally determines (*vaststelt*) income and tax assessments. |
| Social Insurance Bank | *Sociale Verzekeringsbank* (SVB) | Agency responsible for national insurance schemes including old age pension (*AOW*), survivor benefits (*Anw*), and child benefit (*kinderbijslag* / AKW). |
| Employee Insurance Agency | *Uitvoeringsinstituut Werknemersverzekeringen* (UWV) | Agency responsible for employee insurance schemes including unemployment benefit (*WW*) and disability benefit (*WIA*). |
| Information Bureau | *Inlichtingenbureau* | Intermediary that connects municipalities (*gemeenten*) with national data sources for social domain tasks. |

## Temporal and Deadline Concepts

| English | Dutch | Definition |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Deadline / term | *termijn* | A legally defined time period within which an action must be taken. |
| Public holidays | *feestdagen* | Officially recognized holidays listed in the General Deadlines Act (*Algemene Termijnenwet*) article 3. See the Public Holidays section for the full list. |
| Effective date | *inwerkingtredingsdatum* | The date on which a law or regulation takes effect. |
| Publication date | *publicatiedatum* | The date on which a law or regulation is officially published. |
| Calculation date | *berekeningsdatum* | The reference date used for temporal filtering — determines which version of a law applies. |

## Document Structure

| English | Dutch | Definition |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Article | *artikel* | Fundamental unit of a law or regulation. |
| Paragraph | *lid* (plural: *leden*) | Numbered subdivision within an article (e.g., article 3 paragraph 1 = artikel 3 lid 1). |
| Sub-paragraph / part | *onderdeel* | Lettered or numbered subdivision within a paragraph. |
| Preamble | *aanhef* | Opening section of a law, typically containing the enacting formula and references to enabling legislation. |

## Official Publications

| English | Dutch | Definition |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Official Gazette (laws) | *Staatsblad* (Stb.) | Publication channel for formal laws (*wetten*) and general administrative orders (*AMvBen*). |
| Government Gazette | *Staatscourant* (Stcrt.) | Publication channel for ministerial regulations, royal decrees, and other government announcements. |
| Municipal gazette | *gemeenteblad* | Publication channel for municipal regulations and announcements. |
| Provincial gazette | *provincieblad* | Publication channel for provincial regulations and announcements. |

## Public Holidays

| English | Dutch | Definition |
|---------|-------|------------|
| New Year's Day | *Nieuwjaarsdag* | 1 January. |
| King's Day | *Koningsdag* | 27 April (shifted to 26 April when the 27th falls on a Sunday). |
| Liberation Day | *Bevrijdingsdag* | 5 May. |
| Christmas Days | *Kerstdagen* | 25 and 26 December (First and Second Christmas Day). |
| Good Friday | *Goede Vrijdag* | Friday before Easter Sunday. |
| Easter Monday | *Tweede Paasdag* | Monday after Easter Sunday. |
| Ascension Day | *Hemelvaartsdag* | 39 days after Easter Sunday (always a Thursday). |
| Whit Monday | *Tweede Pinksterdag* | 50 days after Easter Sunday. |
| Equivalent days / bridge days | *gelijkgestelde dagen* | Additional days designated by royal decree (*KB*) as equivalent to public holidays for deadline calculation purposes (Algemene Termijnenwet article 3 lid 3). |

## Common Dutch Phrases in Legal Text

| Dutch phrase | English meaning | Context |
|-------------|----------------|---------|
| *Gelet op artikel X van de Y* | In consideration of article X of Y | Preamble formula: a lower regulation states which higher law authorizes it. |
| *In afwijking van artikel X* | In derogation of / Notwithstanding article X | Lex specialis: a specific law overrides a general provision. |
| *Onze Minister* | Our Minister | Formal reference to the responsible minister in law text. |
